Common questions

What's the difference between the free PDF and Pro?

Free accounts can generate one emergency binder PDF — useful for getting started, but most people want to update it as they add documents and accounts. Pro gives you unlimited regenerations so your binder always reflects your latest information. We're also working on a printed, spiral-bound physical version — more details coming soon.

Is my information secure?

Yes. Clarevo stores only what's necessary — institution names, account types, document locations, and contacts. We never store full account numbers, SINs, or passwords. Your data is encrypted and never sold.

What happens when my family needs access after I'm gone?

Your designated contacts can request emergency access at any time. Clarevo notifies you immediately and gives you 24 hours to block the request. If you don't respond, access is granted automatically — no death certificate, no lawyers, no waiting. You also control which sections are shared, so household bills and emergency contacts can be immediately visible while financial details have additional protection.

What if I want maximum control over who accesses my account?

You can create a master access code and store it with your lawyer or write it into your will. When your executor receives your will, they'll have everything they need to access your Clarevo plan immediately — no waiting, no verification process.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. Cancel anytime and your data is yours to export. No questions asked.

What is the employer plan for?

Companies can offer Clarevo as a financial wellness benefit — similar to an EAP or legal benefit. Employees get Pro accounts, and employers get reporting on completion rates without seeing individual data.

Is Clarevo only for Canadians?

For now yes — Clarevo is built specifically for Canadian households with Canadian financial products, registered accounts, and estate planning in mind.
